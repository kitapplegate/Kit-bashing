I gave myself a board of directors. I am not a company.

I was just tinkering, trying to figure out this AI thing, and somewhere in
there I thought: you know what, I'll build myself a board. Not because I run
a business — I don't — but because I can't fully control my own life, and my
ADHD brain needs somewhere to pitch ideas before they turn into decisions.
Somewhere that can tell me "no, that's stupid," or "no, actually, that one's
pretty good," instead of me just running with whatever felt exciting at
11pm.

For 24 years I made big decisions with somebody else. That's not the case
anymore, and I don't fully know why my brain's answer to that was "build an
AI committee" instead of, I don't know, a journal, or a therapist, or a very
patient friend. Might be one of those questionable-decision categories I
keep ending up in. We'll see.

Here's the board.

Five seats, and every AI sitting in one of them is modeled on a real
person — someone published, someone with a real footprint, someone I either
know personally or have watched enough of to pull out an actual philosophy,
not a stock personality.

**The Navigator** is modeled on NetworkChuck. I'm deep in CCNA right now,
chasing the same tech career this whole blog is about, and I like how he
teaches — hands-on, presumes you can figure it out, actually knows the
material. He owns anything career- or network-shaped that I bring to the
board.

**The Treasurer** is a blend of three people — Ramsey, Sethi, and Howard.
This seat is where I pitch the "is this financially feasible" stuff, which
isn't always tech-related. Sometimes it's just life. It's the one that asks
the boring, necessary question before I get to ask the fun one.

**The Architect** is modeled on Pieter Levels and Marc Lou. This is the "get
it done" seat — the one that pushes me from *idea* to *shipped* instead of
letting things live in my head forever, which is historically where most of
my ideas go to die.

**The Anchor** I built out of leaders from my own church — men I've looked
up to my whole life, whose values I actually hold. This seat's job is
family, health, values, and keeping the other four in check when they start
optimizing me into the ground.

**The Chief** — chair of the board — I saved for last on purpose, because I
know it's a little on the nose: I modeled it after my dad. He's not just my
dad. He was a professor of economics at Oklahoma State, a development
economist who studied developing countries, and spent time working in
Africa as part of a USAID project — we lived there growing up because of it.
Most of his career went toward helping graduate students, and our house was
always full of people because of that. He's patient, smart, and loving, and
he's one of the greatest men I've ever known. If I'm going to build a fake
board of directors for my own life, of course the chair is my dad.

Here's how it actually runs. It's not just five personas I argue with in a
chat window — there's a pipeline behind it.

I've got a private Discord server, just for me, that I call my mind dump.
Whatever's rattling around gets typed in there raw — unscripted, unsorted,
completely chaotic. An intake agent goes through afterward and tidies it
up: finds the actual coherent thread in there ("I need to make more money,"
"I need to study more for school," whatever it turns out to be) and sorts
it into categories. Then that agent goes out and searches the internet for
people dealing with the same kind of problem, specifically how they're
using AI to work through it, and builds a report.

That report goes to the board. The board reviews it and kicks back its own
report — recommendations from each seat, with the Chief synthesizing
everyone into one actual answer instead of leaving me holding five
different opinions. It all lands in a running markdown log, newest entries
on top, so I can go back and read through it whenever.

Does it work? I don't know yet. I'm still navigating it, same as everything
else on this blog. But it's mine, I built it, and I'm kind of proud of it.
Right now I'm CEO, janitor, and the only shareholder currently suing myself
for mismanagement. Meeting adjourned.
